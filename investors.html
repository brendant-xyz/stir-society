The business, in plain terms.

The only true comparable in NYC is a beloved 10-seat boutique that fills every cohort — evidence of excess demand, not saturation. No one runs this model at scale, with modern brand and curriculum, and no one runs it in Brooklyn.

The moat

One-off class studios can't retrofit structure: their economics depend on churn-tolerant entertainment. Career schools can't move down-market: their cost base is accreditation and placement. Stir Society's defensibility is operational — a rolling-cohort engine (two stacked 6-session blocks, four nights a week) that keeps a single kitchen at near-full utilization year-round, plus a competency curriculum that takes cycles of cohorts to tune. Same playbook as modern healthcare workforce training, applied to the kitchen.
